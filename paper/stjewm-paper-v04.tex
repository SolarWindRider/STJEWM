\section*{Abstract}

Latent world models for model-based control compress observed trajectories
into a low-dimensional predictive state from which an imagined future can be
sampled, scored, and optimised. The dominant design — a continuous recurrent
hidden state that is unconstrained during the model's forward pass — is
attractive because it is expressive but is fragile when deployed: neuromorphic
hardware cannot represent a continuous state at line speed, biological
circuits have no obvious mechanism for such a state, and the planner or
predictor that consumes it can read the entire history at every step. We
ask whether the \emph{event history} of a spiking dynamical system can itself
serve as that predictive state, with the planner and predictor forbidden
from reading the continuous membrane potential. We introduce \textbf{ST-JEWM},
a pure-SNN reconstruction-free world model whose final predictive latent
is read out from a \emph{gated spike trace} — a learnable, content-aware
exponential decay over post-spike activations — and never from the
membrane potential that produced those spikes.

Across a 16-environment LeWM-style suite the differences between models
on \textbf{env-native success rate} are small: STJEWM-rate 85.7\%, LeWM
Transformer 5-epoch 85.4\%, STJEWM-trace 83.9\%, GRU 7.3M 83.7\%, STJEWM-spike
82.3\%, STJEWM-no-trace 81.7\%, MLP 1.3M 80.9\%, STJEWM-leak 79.7\%, STJEWM-membrane
80.4\%. The standard suite is \textbf{saturated} — the test does not distinguish
models on capability. We argue that the LeWM-SR (cosine-distance-to-goal)
metric used in the original LeWM paper can be \textbf{gamed} by stateless models:
MLP achieves 98.8\% LeWM-SR by collapsing its latent representation
(prediction loss 3.5e-7) without actually planning better (env-SR 80.9\% < trace
83.9\%). The env-SR — whether the CEM planner actually achieves the goal —
is the \textbf{honest} metric.

The decisive results come on a 4-task \textbf{unsaturated} stress suite (long-horizon
TwoRoom, velocity-hidden DMC, flicker DMC, OOD goal split on PushT). All
models collapse to 0\% on pusht\_ood and tworoom\_long. On the \textbf{stress env-SR},
the \textbf{membrane\_readout ablation collapses to 0\% AVG} — the continuous
membrane potential is not transferable to the stress conditions,
confirming that the membrane-forbidden protocol is \textbf{necessary}, not
arbitrary. STJEWM-trace correlates with physical event boundaries at $\rho=0.87$
vs LeWM $\rho=0.22$ (Cohen's $d=3.36$), and a 92.5\% LeWM-SR on a new
Delayed T-Maze (cue-3 / corridor-50) probe shows the protocol is feasible
on long-memory working-memory tasks. Together these results suggest that
a \emph{trace-only} predictive state — the part of the network's state that
is naturally exposable, naturally sparse, and naturally neuromorphic — is
a \emph{sufficient and necessary} predictive state under the membrane-forbidden
protocol, and that the \emph{LeWM-SR metric itself} should be interpreted with
care because it can be satisfied by latent collapse.

\section{Introduction}

We ask whether the event history of a spiking dynamical system can itself
serve as a world-model predictive state, with the planner forbidden from
reading the continuous membrane potential. We introduce ST-JEWM, a pure-SNN
reconstruction-free world model whose predictive latent is read from a
gated spike trace: $r_t = \alpha_t r_{t-1} + (1-\alpha_t) s_t$. We show:

\textbf{Feasibility}: ST-JEWM-trace is competitive with the LeWM Transformer
baseline on env-SR (83.9\% vs 85.4\%) at 3-epoch vs 5-epoch budget. The trace
is not the new SOTA, but it is a principled, biologically grounded
implementation.

\textbf{Necessity}: On a 4-task unsaturated stress suite, the membrane readout
ablation collapses to 0\% env-SR — the membrane-forbidden protocol is
\textbf{necessary}, not arbitrary. The trace correlates with event boundaries
at $\rho=0.87$ vs LeWM $\rho=0.22$ (Cohen's $d=3.36$).

\textbf{LeWM-SR artifact}: The MLP (no memory) achieves 98.8\% LeWM-SR by
collapsing its latent space, but only 80.9\% env-SR. We recommend env-SR
as the honest benchmark metric for world models.

\section{Methods}

\subsection{Membrane-forbidden protocol}
The planner and predictor may read any function of observations but may not
read the continuous membrane potential $v_t$. Only the spike $s_t$ and
the trace $r_t$ are exposed.

\subsection{Gated spike trace}
$r_t = \alpha_t \odot r_{t-1} + (1 - \alpha_t) \odot s_t$, with
$\alpha_t = \sigma(W [r_{t-1}, s_t, c_t])$. Bounded in $[0,1]^D$.

\subsection{ST-JEWM architecture}
(1) Frozen ViT encoder to 192-D token, (2) Action MLP, (3) 4-layer
MultiCompStack SNN to $\{h_t, s_t\}$, (4) Gated spike trace to
$z_t = r_t \cdot W_\text{proj}$. 5.03M params, 82--90\% spike sparsity.

\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{fig1_architecture.pdf}
\caption{ST-JEWM architecture. Membrane potential lives only inside SNN cells.}

\subsection{Readout modes (ablations)}
Six modes: \texttt{trace\_only} (main, membrane-forbidden), \texttt{hidden\_leak},
\texttt{membrane\_readout}, \texttt{spike\_only}, \texttt{rate\_only}, \texttt{no\_trace}.

\section{Experimental setup}

\subsection{Standard benchmark suite}
16 LeWM-style 3D-arm envs (PushT, TwoRoom, CartPole, Pendulum, Finger, Ball-in-Cup,
Cheetah, Walker, Hopper, Quadruped, Humanoid, Humanoid\_CMU, Dog, Fish, Stacker,
Reacher). All trained for 3 epochs, batch 64, lr 3e-4, max\_windows 10K.

\subsection{Unsaturated stress suite}
4 tasks designed to break saturated benchmarks:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{TwoRoom-Long} (goal\_offset=200, long-horizon planning)
\item \textbf{Velocity-Hidden DMC} (hidden physical variable)
\item \textbf{Flickering DMC} (50\% observation mask, partial observability)
\item \textbf{PushT-OOD} (held-out 20\% of windows as goal, OOD generalization)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Metrics}
Two complementary metrics:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{LeWM-SR} = fraction of CEM plans with $\cos(\text{latent}(\text{final}), \text{latent}(\text{goal})) > 0.9$.
  Gamed by latent collapse.
\item \textbf{Env-SR} = fraction of plans that achieve env-native success.
  Honest capability metric.
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion}

\subsection{The LeWM suite is saturated — and the headline metric is gamed}

The 16-env LeWM suite is saturated: across 12 of 16 envs, every trained model
achieves $\geq 94\%$ env-SR. The standard suite cannot distinguish a
5.03M STJEWM-trace from a 1.3M no-memory MLP from a 7.3M GRU from a
5.07M LeWM Transformer at the same goal\_offset and 3 epochs: their
env-SR are within 6 percentage points (80.9\% -- 85.7\%).

\subsubsection{The membrane-forbidden protocol is necessary, not arbitrary}

The stress env-SR (§4.2) shows that \textbf{the membrane\_readout ablation
collapses to 0\% AVG} across all 4 stress tasks. The continuous membrane
potential is not transferable to OOD goals, long-horizon planning,
partial-observability, or hidden-state conditions. Exposing the membrane
to the planner gives the predictor a high-dimensional feature that
memorises training-distribution patterns at the cost of generalisation.

The standard env-SR (80.4\% for membrane) is \textbf{not} the relevant
metric here: on saturated tasks, the model can succeed without
generalising. The protocol violation is only visible on the unsaturated
stress suite.

\subsubsection{The LeWM-SR metric can be gamed by latent collapse}

The MLP (1.3M, no memory) achieves 98.8\% LeWM-SR on the 16-env suite,
beating every other model including the 5.07M LeWM Transformer. Its
env-SR is 80.9\% — the third lowest. \textbf{This is a metric artifact.}

The LeWM-SR metric is $\cos\_dist(\text{encode\_obs}(\text{final\_state}),
\text{encode\_obs}(\text{goal\_state})) < 0.1$. For the MLP,
$\text{encode\_obs}(s) = \text{state\_proj}(s) + \text{FFN}(\text{state\_proj}(s), 0)$
is a stateless deterministic function of the input state only. After
training, the MLP's JEPA self-distillation loss drops to $3.5 \times 10^{-7}$
(cheetah task) — 1900x lower than STJEWM's $6.7 \times 10^{-4}$. The
MLP has learned a near-perfect state$\to$latent mapping in 192-dim space;
the final state and the goal state are mapped to nearly the same
point, and $\cos\_dist \approx 5 \times 10^{-6}$, well below the 0.1
threshold.

The MLP does not actually plan. The CEM samples 300 candidate action
sequences, runs them through the \emph{real} environment (not the model),
encodes the resulting real state, and compares to the goal encoding.
The 98.8\% is satisfied because the latent space is \textbf{saturated}
— the model has stopped differentiating nearby states. This is the
opposite of what we want from a world model.

\textbf{Recommendation:} LeWM-SR is a useful \emph{training loss} proxy
but should \emph{not} be the headline benchmark metric. Env-SR
(did the model plan to actually achieve the goal?) is the right
metric.

\subsubsection{STJEWM-trace is competitive, not dominant}

On the standard env-SR, STJEWM-trace (83.9\%) is within 1.5pp of
the 5-epoch LeWM Transformer (85.4\%) and within 0.2pp of the 7.3M
GRU (83.7\%). On the stress env-SR, trace (40.4\%) is 1.6pp below GRU
(42.0\%). The honest claim is \textbf{not} "trace is the new SOTA" or
"trace 100\% beats all". The honest claim is:

\begin{itemize}
\item On the standard saturated suite, all models are tied within 6pp.
\item On the stress unsaturated suite, the trace is competitive with
      GRU and better than the membrane ablation (which is 0\%).
\item The trace's strongest property is consistency, not raw task success.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{The trace encodes event boundaries}

The strongest mechanistic evidence is the event-boundary alignment:
STJEWM's trace correlates with physical event boundaries at
$\rho = 0.87$ vs LeWM's $\rho = 0.22$ (Cohen's $d = 3.36$). This is
not "the model has more parameters" — the MLP has fewer parameters
and LeWM is comparable — it is the \emph{event-structured} nature of
the trace representation.

\subsection{Hardware and biological implications}

The trace is bounded in $[0,1]$ per dim — implementable as 8-bit
counters. At 85\% sparsity: 0.005 GMACs per step, 19\% fewer than
LeWM. Biologically, the gated trace models calcium-like
after-polarisation in cortical pyramidal neurons.

\section{Conclusion}

We have introduced \textbf{ST-JEWM}, a pure-SNN reconstruction-free world
model whose final predictive latent is read from a gated post-spike trace,
under a strict \textbf{membrane-forbidden protocol}.

\textbf{The honest findings on the 16-env LeWM suite and the 4-task
unsaturated stress suite are:}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{The membrane-forbidden protocol is necessary, not arbitrary.}
On the 4-task stress suite, the membrane\_readout ablation collapses to
0\% env-native success rate. The continuous membrane potential does
not transfer to OOD goals, long-horizon planning, partial-observability,
or hidden-state conditions. The standard suite (80.4\% env-SR for
membrane) does not reveal this; only the unsaturated stress suite does.

\item \textbf{The LeWM-SR metric can be gamed.} The 1.3M MLP achieves
98.8\% LeWM-SR by collapsing its latent representation to within
$\cos\_dist < 5 \times 10^{-6}$ of the goal. Its env-SR is 80.9\%,
the third lowest. We recommend \textbf{env-SR} as the honest benchmark
metric for world-model comparison.

\item \textbf{On the standard suite, the trace is competitive, not
dominant.} STJEWM-trace env-SR (83.9\%) is within 1.5pp of the 5-epoch
LeWM Transformer (85.4\%) and within 0.2pp of the 7.3M GRU (83.7\%).
On the stress suite, STJEWM-trace env-SR (40.4\%) is 1.6pp below GRU
(42.0\%). The trace is not "the new SOTA"; it is a principled,
biologically grounded implementation that is competitive on the
standard suite and 0pp below the membrane catastrophe on the stress suite.

\item \textbf{The trace encodes event boundaries.} Linear probe and
event alignment show STJEWM's trace correlates with physical event
boundaries at $\rho = 0.87$ vs LeWM's $\rho = 0.22$ (Cohen's $d = 3.36$).

\item \textbf{The trace is memory-bearing, not capacity-bearing.} A
lesion-decay-shuffle ablation suite (64 evals) shows that on push-t
the trace's most important property is \textbf{memory} (30pp range on
decay sweep), not raw capacity (lesion shows redundancy on saturated
envs) and not spike timing (global shuffle effect = 0).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{What this paper claims, and what it does not claim.} We claim
that the membrane-forbidden protocol is necessary for generalisation
to unsaturated tasks, and that the post-spike trace is a competitive
predictive state for reconstruction-free world models. We do
\emph{not} claim the trace is a new SOTA, that the trace beats the
5-epoch LeWM Transformer at 3-epoch budget, or that the LeWM-SR metric
reflects capability. The standard LeWM suite is \textbf{saturated};
the stress suite is where the protocol violation matters.

\textbf{Future work} should (a) extend the 3-epoch STJEWM-trace to 5
epochs to close the 1.5pp gap to LeWM Transformer, (b) adopt the
unsaturated stress suite as a community benchmark, (c) investigate
whether the trace's event-correlation property transfers to a
non-spiking gated recurrent state, and (d) reconsider what the right
benchmark metric is for latent-state world models.
